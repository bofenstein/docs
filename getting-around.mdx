This guide walks you through the main screens and controls in 2du so you can start finding local content, exploring new places, and getting the most out of the app from day one.

## Main screens

<Steps>
  <Step title="Home screen">
    When you open 2du, you land on the home screen showing the top local content for your current location. You'll see up to 200 results pulled from platforms like YouTube, TripAdvisor, Yelp, local news outlets, Airbnb, Booking.com, Viator, and more — all anchored to where you are right now.

    The results update automatically whenever you move the map to a new location.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Moving the map">
    Tap and drag the map to explore any city, region, or country in the world. As you move the map cursor to a new location, the content feed on the home screen updates to show resources relevant to that place.

    You can explore Tokyo hotels, Buenos Aires restaurants, or a national park trailhead — all without leaving the app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browsing content cards">
    Scroll through the feed to see location-specific content cards. Each card represents a website, video, blog post, review, or booking page tied to your selected location. Tap any card to open it directly in the app.

    Cards can include travel videos, restaurant reviews, local news articles, accommodation listings, experience guides, and business pages.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map search">
    Use the search feature to find specific businesses, attractions, or categories near any map location. Search by name, category, keyword, or brand. Results appear as pins on the map. Tap a pin to see a summary, then tap again for one-tap streetview and directions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Favorites">
    Tap the save icon on any business or location to add it to your favorites. Access your saved places anytime from the favorites tab so you can quickly get back to spots you want to revisit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use 2du to research a destination before you travel. Move the map to your destination city and browse the local content feed — find restaurants, read reviews, watch travel videos, and book accommodation or experiences — all in one place before you arrive.
</Tip>
